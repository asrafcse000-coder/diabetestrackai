About Diabetes Track AI

Learn about our advanced machine learning model and how it helps assess diabetes risk.

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic disease that affects how your body regulates blood glucose levels. When you eat, your body breaks down food into glucose and uses it for energy. The pancreas produces insulin, which helps cells absorb glucose.

Type 2 Diabetes occurs when the body cannot effectively use the insulin produced (insulin resistance) or doesn't produce enough insulin. It accounts for about 90% of all diabetes cases.

Early detection and management of diabetes risk factors can significantly improve health outcomes and prevent or delay complications.

How Our ML Model Works

Data Input

The model analyzes 13 key health parameters including age, BMI, blood glucose, HbA1c levels, blood pressure, heart disease history, and smoking status.

Processing

A Hyper Parameter tuned Random Forest classifier processes your data using patterns learned from thousands of clinical cases to identify diabetes risk factors.

Analysis

The model evaluates how your metrics compare to healthy ranges and calculates a probability score reflecting your diabetes risk level.

Report

You receive a comprehensive medical-style report with your assessment results, personalized recommendations, and printable documentation.

Technical Details

Algorithm Random Forest Classifier (Tuned)
Input Features 13 clinical and demographic parameters
Output Binary classification (Diabetic/Non-Diabetic) with probability score
Use Case Educational & Research - NOT a clinical diagnostic tool
Accuracy Validated on multiple test datasets with high precision

Key Risk Factors Assessed

Blood Glucose
Higher fasting glucose levels indicate reduced glucose control
BMI
Higher BMI increases insulin resistance and diabetes risk
HbA1c Level
Reflects 3-month average glucose; higher levels indicate poor control
Age
Risk increases with age, especially after 45 years
Heart Disease
Significant correlation with diabetes and metabolic disease
Smoking
Smoking impairs glucose metabolism and increases insulin resistance
